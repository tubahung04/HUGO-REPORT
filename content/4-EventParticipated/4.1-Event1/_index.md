### Key Highlights

#### 1. Context Is Everything: Making AI Actually Work for You
**Speaker:** Pham Ng Hai Anh – AWS Community Builder

This session emphasized the importance of Context Engineering in maximizing AI effectiveness. Through the introduction of Amazon Quick, attendees explored an Agentic AI platform designed to help organizations leverage data and automate business workflows.

**Key Features of Amazon Quick:**
- **Multi-source Data Integration:** Connects with more than 40 data sources, documents, and enterprise databases.
- **Workflow Automation:** Supports meeting summary generation, email creation, scheduling, and data analysis.
- **Intelligent Orchestration:** Automatically selects appropriate workflows and triggers APIs using natural language.

#### 2. Amazon CloudFront – Foundation from Edge to Origin
**Speaker:** Nguyen Tuan Thinh – DevOps Engineer

The presentation highlighted Amazon CloudFront as a critical service for improving performance, strengthening security, and reducing operational costs.

**Key Benefits:**
- Reduces workload on origin servers, leading to lower infrastructure costs.
- Enhances security through the combination of Origin Access Control (OAC) and Origin Shield.
- Protects services such as Amazon S3 and API Gateway from unauthorized access.
- Improves application performance and reliability at scale.

#### 3. Context Is Everything – Making AI Truly Effective
**Speaker:** Tinh Truong – Platform Engineer, GoTymeX

The presentation provided practical insights into improving AI effectiveness through proper context design. It emphasized that the quality of context is more important than the quantity of information provided to an AI model. Common mistakes in prompt creation and AI interactions were analyzed and discussed.

**A four-component framework was introduced to optimize AI outputs:**
- Goal
- Relevant Information
- Constraints
- Success Criteria

The session highlighted the importance of Context Engineering in enhancing AI performance and reliability. It also explored the evolution of AI from Prompt Engineering to Context Engineering, and ultimately to Memory-based AI (Second AI Brain). The speaker outlined a vision for future AI systems that can retain memory and provide long-term personalized assistance to users.

#### 4. Enterprise-Grade Multi-Agent System
**Speaker:** Vy Lam – Senior Business Systems Analyst, VPBank

The presentation focused on the application of Multi-Agent AI Architecture in startup credit assessment. This is a challenging problem because startups often lack sufficient data and evaluation criteria compared to traditional businesses.

**Key Challenges:**
- Limited credit history and reliable financial data.
- Difficulty in assessing the value of collateral.
- Data collected from multiple sources with inconsistent formats.
- Manual evaluation processes that require significant time and resources.

**Proposed Solution:**
- Develop a system consisting of multiple specialized AI agents, each responsible for a specific task.
- Simultaneously analyze factors such as financial performance, market potential, team capability, and business risks.
- Combine insights from multiple agents to produce a comprehensive and objective assessment.

**Benefits Achieved:**
- Reduced credit evaluation and approval time.
- Improved accuracy in the assessment process.
- Enhanced transparency and explainability of decisions.
- Increased system resilience by ensuring that failures in one component do not affect the entire system.

#### Enterprise AI & Security + LLM Non-Determinism
**Speaker:** Duc Dao – Solutions Architect, Cloud Kinetics. 

The session focused on the key requirements for deploying AI in enterprise environments, emphasizing not only performance but also reliability, security, and scalability.

**Core Requirements:**
- **Secure:** Protect data and ensure information security.
- **Reliable:** Maintain system stability and minimize operational errors. 
- **Scalable:** Support increasing workloads and growing business demands.
- **Compliant:** Adhere to organizational policies, regulations, and industry standards.

**LLM Non-Determinism:**
Large Language Models (LLMs) may produce different outputs even when given the same input and a temperature setting of 0. This behavior is caused by factors such as floating-point computations on GPUs, parallel execution processes, and batching mechanisms during inference.

**Mitigation Strategies:**
- Standardize responses using Structured Output.
- Apply Majority Voting to improve result reliability.
- Combine multiple models through an Ensemble Approach.
- Establish continuous testing and evaluation processes before deployment into production environments.

#### 6. Hackathon Project – UTMorpho
**Speakers:** Thao Nguyen, Mai Nguyen, and Uyen Le – GenAI Engineer, VIB.

This session introduced UTMorpho, a project developed during the LotusHacks Hackathon, aimed at simplifying user interface design through AI-powered assistance.

**Key Features:**
- Generate user interfaces directly from natural language descriptions.
- Enable real-time UI editing on the design canvas.
- Reduce the need for repeatedly rewriting prompts.
- Maintain design consistency throughout the editing process.

**Development Challenges:**
- Token limitations when processing large codebases.
- Difficulty controlling the amount of AI-generated code.
- Tight time constraints, with only 36 hours available to complete the project.
- High work intensity during the hackathon, leading to fatigue and reduced productivity.
