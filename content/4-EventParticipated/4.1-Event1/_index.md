# Reflection Report – AWS Vietnam Community Day 2026

### Purpose of the Event
AWS Vietnam Community Day 2026 was organized to provide participants with practical knowledge of AWS and the latest trends in Cloud Computing through presentations from industry experts and the AWS community. Besides updating technical knowledge, the event also focused on developing professional thinking, soft skills, and adaptability in today’s technology environment.

**The main objectives of the event were:**
* Provide guidance on learning AWS and developing cloud-related skills.
* Help participants understand the causes of procrastination and build effective working habits.
* Improve confidence, communication, and presentation skills in technical environments.
* Share practical experiences from Hackathons and product development.
* Highlight the importance of DevOps and software development processes in reducing project risks.
* Introduce scalable cloud architectures for modern web and mobile applications.

### Speakers
* **Huynh Thai Linh** – Technical Speaker, First Cloud AI Journey
* **Khac Uy** – 3rd Year Student, Vietnamese-German University
* **Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu** – Why We Always Need Confidence
* **Pham Quang Thai** – Cloud Architecture for Multiplayer Games
* **Tran Huu Nghia** – Full-Stack Developer
* **Nguyen Tran Minh Quan** – DevOps & Project Management
* **The Ballers Team (FPT University)** – Hackathon Sharing Session

---

### Key Sessions

#### 1. Level Up Your AWS Skills with Cloud Pathways
**Speaker:** Huynh Thai Linh
The speaker introduced an effective AWS learning roadmap for beginners by combining theoretical knowledge with hands-on practice. **Floci** was presented as a local AWS simulation tool that allows developers to test applications without incurring cloud costs. In addition, **AWS Cloud Quest** provides an interactive game-based learning experience that helps users understand AWS services more easily.

**The recommended learning process consists of three stages:**
1. Learn AWS concepts through Cloud Quest.
2. Practice locally using Floci.
3. Deploy applications to the real AWS environment.

#### 2. The Iceberg of Procrastination
**Speaker:** Khac Uy
This session explained that procrastination is usually caused by psychological factors such as fear of failure, anxiety, or perfectionism rather than laziness. The speaker suggested breaking large tasks into smaller and more manageable ones to reduce pressure and make it easier to take action. This approach is especially useful when working on large software projects.

#### 3. Why We Always Need Confidence
**Speaker:** Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu
The presentation emphasized that confidence plays an important role in both learning and professional development. According to the speaker, technical knowledge becomes more valuable when people can communicate clearly, present their ideas, and express their opinions confidently.

One of the most interesting topics was **Imposter Syndrome**, a psychological condition in which people doubt their own abilities despite their achievements. Many students and young professionals believe their success comes only from luck and worry that others will eventually discover they are “not good enough.”

The speaker encouraged participants to recognize their achievements, ask questions without hesitation, continue learning, and view mistakes as opportunities for improvement. This session reminded me that confidence is a skill that can be developed through continuous practice and experience.

#### 4. The Ballers – Hackathon: More Like “HA! A Tons of Fun”
**Speaker:** The Ballers Team
The Ballers Team shared their journey of participating in a Hackathon, from generating ideas and assigning tasks to completing a product within a limited time. Through their experiences, they emphasized that teamwork, adaptability, and determination are just as important as technical skills.

The speakers also encouraged students to participate in Hackathons to gain practical experience, improve problem-solving skills, and challenge themselves in real development environments.

#### 5. How Cloud Architecture Powers Modern Multiplayer Games
**Speaker:** Pham Quang Thai
The speaker explained how large-scale online games are built on cloud platforms to support thousands of concurrent players. AWS services such as Amazon GameLift, Amazon DynamoDB, and Amazon CloudFront were introduced to manage game servers, store player data, and optimize network performance.

This session demonstrated the importance of cloud architecture in building highly available and scalable gaming systems.

#### 6. The Hidden Iceberg of a Project
**Speaker:** Nguyen Tran Minh Quan
Using the Iceberg Model, the speaker explained that project failures are often caused not only by coding issues but also by hidden problems such as communication, collaboration, testing, and development processes.

DevOps was introduced as an effective approach that improves collaboration, automates workflows, accelerates feedback, and continuously enhances software quality.

#### 7. A Comprehensive Astrology Platform for the New Generation
**Speaker:** Tran Huu Nghia
The speaker presented the development of a modern astrology platform that combines Cloud Computing and Artificial Intelligence. AWS services were used to process data, provide personalized experiences, and improve system scalability.

This project demonstrated that cloud technologies can be applied to many industries beyond traditional enterprise applications.

### What I Learned

**Technical Perspective**
* Better understood an effective AWS learning roadmap that combines theory with practice.
* Recognized the importance of DevOps in improving software quality.
* Learned how Floci can reduce AWS practice costs.
* Understood how Cloud Computing and AI support scalable application development.
* Gained more knowledge about designing cloud architectures for high-traffic systems.

**Personal Development**
Besides technical knowledge, the event also provided valuable lessons for personal growth:
* Procrastination is usually caused by fear rather than a lack of ability.
* Confidence is a skill that can be developed through continuous practice.
* It is important to identify the root causes of problems instead of focusing only on their symptoms.
* Communication and teamwork are essential skills for technology professionals.

**Practical Skills**
After attending the event, I learned:
* How to divide large tasks into smaller and more manageable ones.
* The importance of teamwork in high-pressure environments.
* Better presentation and communication skills.
* Practical experience in completing projects within limited time.

After attending the event, I plan to use **AWS Cloud Quest** to improve my AWS knowledge and **Floci** to test applications before deploying them to the AWS cloud. I will also apply task management techniques by dividing large projects into smaller tasks, communicating more actively with teammates, and improving my presentation skills.

### Event Experience
AWS Vietnam Community Day 2026 provided me with valuable technical knowledge and inspiring experiences from industry professionals. I was particularly impressed by the combination of technical sessions, such as Cloud Architecture and DevOps, with topics related to confidence, communication, and personal development.

The friendly atmosphere encouraged participants to exchange ideas, ask questions, and learn from real-world experiences shared by the speakers.

### Key Takeaways
After attending the event, I realized that a successful software project depends not only on technical knowledge but also on effective teamwork and well-defined development processes. I also learned that confidence can be built through continuous learning and the willingness to take on new challenges.

In addition, participating in activities such as Hackathons is an excellent opportunity to gain practical experience, improve problem-solving abilities, and strengthen teamwork skills.

Overall, AWS Vietnam Community Day 2026 expanded my understanding of Cloud Computing while motivating me to continue learning, practicing, and preparing for a future career in cloud technology.
